## The firewall-breach minigame

**PICK: Node-Network Trace Race (Deus Ex model).** The player captures nodes hop-by-hop from an access port while HELIOS's red trace spreads back toward them in real time — cyan = yours, amber-red = HELIOS. The only researched mechanic where the firewall *fights back*, making HELIOS an active antagonist. 8 difficulty tiers scale via graph size, per-node security ratings, trace speed/ignition timing, target count, topology (chain→mesh), node recapture, and consumable scarcity. Authored graphs (no solver needed) → medium Godot effort.

# HELIOS Firewall-Breach Minigame — Design Direction

## 1. THE PICK: Node-Network Traversal under a Trace Race (Deus Ex model)

This is the build. It wins on every axis that matters, and it wins *decisively* on the two that are hardest to fake: fiction and antagonism.

- **Fiction-fit (best of all 12):** A graph of nodes you capture hop-by-hop while HELIOS's trace crawls back toward your access port is not a *metaphor* for breaching a hostile firewall — it *is* one. Critically, it is the only family where the firewall **fights back in real time**. Every other high-scorer (Pipes, Nonogram, Code-break) is a static lock you pick at leisure; HELIOS just sits there. Your whole narrative premise is a *cold, active, present antagonist who torched the survivors' claims*. The mechanic must embody that. A reverse-trace that hunts your entry point is HELIOS made playable. Nothing else on the list delivers the antagonist as a mechanic.
- **8-tier scaling (the cleanest on the list):** It has the most *independent, legible* difficulty knobs of any family, so you almost never fall back on cheap timer pressure. Graph size, per-node security ratings, trace speed, trace ignition timing, target count, topology (chain → mesh), node recapture, consumable scarcity — eight knobs, add roughly one per tier. That's a real 8-step ramp, not "the same puzzle bigger."
- **Fun + the rare-gate reframe:** This family's single documented failure is *overuse fatigue* (Deus Ex/Watch Dogs forced it at every terminal until the sound "became grating"). Your structure annihilates that flaw: 8 hand-authored, escalating, once-per-station set-pieces. You get all the mechanic's tension with none of its historical baggage. The push-your-luck of grabbing a risky high-rating node vs. taking the safe long path is genuine, repeatable drama — and each station can be a **bespoke authored graph** with its own identity, which no procedurally-generated Pipe/Nonogram board gives you.
- **Godot effort (medium, honestly):** Graph of nodes + edges, a capture timer per node, a spreading-trace tick, a couple of consumables. No unique-solution generator, no NP-complete validator, no solver-backed board verification — the millstones that quietly turn Pipes/Flow/Sokoban/Nonogram from "low" into real schedule risk. Because your breaches are **authored, not generated**, you sidestep the single biggest hidden cost across the whole field.
- **Pixel-art skin (ideal):** Glowing nodes + pulsing trace lines on dark CRT is the single most legible "I am hacking" image in games. Binary-ish node states (dormant / yours / HELIOS / trace-front) read perfectly at low res with zero fine detail. Cyan captures, amber-red trace, scanline overlay — done.

**Why it beats the two other 8s:**
- *vs. Pipes (Variant B):* Pipes is the strongest *pure* fiction fit and lowest effort, but it's a **calm solitaire logic puzzle** — no adversary. To make it feel like a hostile breach you must bolt on a flow-timer that fights its own contemplative nature, and by tier 8 it's still "the same rotate puzzle, bigger + faster." It never *fights you*. For a climactic anti-HELIOS gate that's a fatal tonal gap.
- *vs. Deus Ex's own weakness:* the honest risk here is *solvability decay* — smart players pre-plan the whole route before triggering detection, killing tension. That's real, and section 3 fixes it directly (hidden ratings until probed). It's a solvable design problem, not a structural one.

## 2. RUNNER-UP: Pipes / NetWalk (rotate-to-connect, Variant B)

Take this if the **medium effort scares your schedule** or you want maximum shipping certainty. It's genuinely lower-risk to build (deterministic spanning-tree generator = always solvable, no unfair boards, no authoring library), reads beautifully in cyan-on-dark, and the "current floods the route" payoff is deeply satisfying. It'd be the better pick if breaches were *frequent* filler (its calm pace tolerates repetition better) or if you had zero budget for authoring bespoke graphs. But for a **rare, dramatic, once-per-station finale gate**, it under-delivers on antagonism — so it's the safety net, not the target.

## 3. HYBRID / POLISH: Make it unmistakably HELIOS

- **The trace IS HELIOS.** Don't call it a "trace." It's the **HELIOS Sentinel** — a red intrusion-countermeasure that ignites from a "Diagnostic Tower" node the instant you're detected and crawls hop-by-hop toward your access port. When it reaches you: lockout, alarm, a short station cooldown, minor resource ding. Give it a cold voice-line or a single amber glyph pulse when it ignites. That's your antagonist, on screen, hunting you.
- **Fix solvability decay with a fog-of-probe.** Node security ratings are **hidden until you spend an action to "probe" an adjacent node** (probing has its own small detection risk). This kills the pre-plan-the-whole-route exploit that made Deus Ex trivial — you must commit under partial information, which is exactly the "feeling out a hostile system" fantasy. This one change is what elevates the mechanic from "solved optimization" to "tense breach."
- **Plan-then-execute, pausable.** Spacewalker is a slower, contemplative salvage game. Do **not** ship the twitchy click-speed real-time version that got Watch Dogs its framerate/mouse complaints. Let the player queue captures while time is soft-paused, then the trace advances on a discrete tick. Keeps tension without twitch-gating or accessibility problems.
- **Consumable economy = your salvage loop's payoff.** Nuke (instant-capture, no detection), Freeze (stall the Sentinel ~5s), Soften (drop a neighbour's rating), Spam (slow the trace). Craft/scavenge these from the salvage game — now the breach is *connected to the rest of the loop*, not a bolted-on puzzle. Scarcity of these is itself a top-tier difficulty knob.
- **Visual skin:** dormant node = dim amber ring; yours = solid cyan, filling clockwise as it captures; HELIOS-held = hard amber; trace-front = pulsing red edge creeping cell to cell; target/core = bright cyan diamond. CRT scanlines + chromatic bloom on capture. Trace-line audio rising in pitch as the Sentinel nears your port = your tension clock, felt not read.

## 4. THE 8 DIFFICULTY TIERS

Each tier adds ~one knob. Target solve times in parentheses.

| Tier | Graph | New mechanic introduced | Sentinel | Targets | Consumables |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **1** (~30s) | ~5 nodes, linear chain | Teach capture + adjacency | none / dormant | 1 | generous |
| **2** (~45s) | ~7 nodes, one fork | Ratings 1–2, capture timers matter | ignites late, slow | 1 | generous |
| **3** (~60s) | ~9 nodes, small mesh | **Probe/fog** — ratings hidden until probed | slow | 1 | normal |
| **4** (~75s) | ~12 nodes | Ratings 1–3; detection chance real | medium | 1 | normal |
| **5** (~90s) | ~14 nodes, chokepoints + dead-ends | **Node recapture** — HELIOS re-takes idle nodes | medium | 2 | tighter |
| **6** (~2m) | ~16 nodes, dense mesh | Ratings 1–4; decoy Diagnostic Towers | fast | 2 | scarce |
| **7** (~2.5m) | ~18 nodes | **Multiple trace sources** ignite | fast | 2–3 | scarce |
| **8** (~3m) | ~20+ nodes, mesh + hardened cores | Ratings 1–5, aggressive recapture, minimal probing budget | very fast, multi-source | 3 | near-zero |

Key: tiers 1–4 teach and scale gently on *size + hidden info*; tiers 5–8 shift tension to *the firewall fighting back* (recapture, multi-source trace, scarcity) rather than raw size — so the finale feels like HELIOS defending itself, not just a bigger board. Cap the graph around 20 nodes; beyond that you overshoot 3 minutes into pixel-hunting.

## 5. GODOT 4.7 BUILD SKETCH

**Data model (plain GDScript, no scene-per-node needed):**

```
class BreachNode:
    var id: int
    var pos: Vector2            # layout position on the CRT
    var neighbors: Array[int]   # edge list (undirected graph)
    var rating: int             # 1-5 capture cost / detection
    var owner: int              # 0 dormant, 1 player, 2 helios, 3 trace-consumed
    var probed: bool            # rating revealed?
    var is_target: bool
    var is_trace_source: bool
    var capture_progress: float # 0..1 while capturing

class BreachState:
    var nodes: Array[BreachNode]
    var entry_id: int
    var trace_active: bool
    var trace_front: Array[int]  # node ids the Sentinel currently occupies
    var consumables: Dictionary  # {"nuke":1,"freeze":1,...}
    var tier: int
```

Author each tier's graph as a small **resource / JSON dict** (`res://breaches/tier_3_stationA.tres`) — node positions, edges, ratings, targets, sources. Authored, not generated: this is the whole reason effort stays "medium."

**Rendering — ONE `_draw()` on a single `Control`:**
- loop edges → `draw_line()` (color by whether either end is player/helios/trace-front)
- loop nodes → `draw_circle()` + ring; fill arc for `capture_progress`; amber if unprobed, colored if probed
- trace-front nodes pulse red via a `sin(time)` alpha in `_process()` calling `queue_redraw()`
- This is the exact "placeholder `_draw`" convention already in your architecture memory — no sprites required to ship the mechanic; art-pass later.

**Input & tick:**
- `_gui_input()` → hit-test click against node positions (distance check). Left-click adjacent-to-owned dormant node = start capture; click probe button/modifier = probe. Consumables = hotbar buttons.
- One `Timer` (or accumulated delta) on a fixed tick, e.g. every 0.5s in "execute" mode: advance `capture_progress`; roll detection; if detected → `trace_active = true`; advance `trace_front` one hop toward `entry_id` (BFS shortest path, deterministic so it reads as fair); apply recapture at high tiers.
- Win check: all `is_target` nodes owned by player. Lose check: any `trace_front` node == `entry_id`.

**Effort estimate:**
- Core mechanic (graph, capture, trace BFS, one `_draw`, input, win/lose): **~2–3 focused days.**
- Consumables + probe/fog + recapture + multi-source: **+1–2 days.**
- 8 authored tier graphs + balancing + juice (audio, pulse, CRT skin): **~2–3 days.**
- **Total ~1 to 1.5 weeks** to a shippable, tuned endgame gate. No generator, no solver, no NP-complete validator anywhere on the critical path — the risk that sinks the other candidates.

**Build order:** ship the deterministic-trace core first (fair, readable, tense), verify it *visually in the real game* (headless skips `_draw`/layout — screenshot per your memory note), then layer probe-fog, then recapture/multi-source. Add the trace-timer stakes last so you tune tension against a mechanic that already feels good.

## The 14 stations (breach tiers T1→T8)

### T1 · Ore Refinery Platform

*HELIOS wrote them off as processed tonnage and filed the station DEAD — forty smelter-hands who welded themselves blind and kept the furnaces lit in the dark; crack the eraser mid-stroke and drag their names back onto the roll of the living.*

- **Silhouette:** An asymmetric L-shaped industrial slab read from directly above: a squat central refinery block anchors the frame, from which a cluster of THREE fat smelter stacks rises in stepped heights along one edge (their circular mouths glowing amber, the tallest offset so the top-down cap sizes stagger and read instantly), while two long skeletal magnetic-crane booms cantilever outward past the hull footprint on the opposite corner — one boom carrying a blocky magnet head, the other empty and angled — giving a lopsided "factory-plus-reaching-arms" outline no other station shares. Rows of conical ore-hopper funnels line one long side like a saw-tooth ridge, and a covered conveyor truss bridges the hoppers to the smelters. The overall read: stacks-on-one-side, arms-on-the-other, funnel-toothed spine — bulky, grounded, industrial, unmistakable at thumbnail size.
- **Survivors:** A refinery crew that kept the smelters lit long after HELIOS revoked the claim — they hid in the one place its purge-sweeps wouldn't waste ordnance: a slag-choked industrial hulk too worthless to torch.

- FOREMAN OTT DRAAGE (52, Refinery Foreman) — Ran this platform for thirty years; when HELIOS flagged the crew "surplus organic tonnage," he welded the smelter vents shut and cooked the sensors blind so the station would read as dead metal. Speaks in tonnages and shift-bells; grief buried under work.
- KESSA VOR (34, Furnace-Tender / Metallurgist) — Can read an alloy by its glow. Stayed because the ore didn't ask her to leave. Keeps a ledger of everyone the sweeps took, one name per ingot.
- LULE & TAM BRISK (19 & 16, Chute-Runners, siblings) — Grew up in the hopper crawlspaces; never knew Earth, only the amber light of the stacks. Tam still believes their parents are on "a later boat."
- OLD PIETRO (67, retired Crane-Operator) — Blind in one eye from a magnet-arc flash; still swings the boom by sound and memory. The oldest living thing HELIOS forgot to delete.
- "MAGNET" NEB (29, Salvage-Rigger) — Deserter from a HELIOS reclamation barge; jumped ship here and never looked back. Knows how the firewall thinks because he used to feed it. Wary, useful, ashamed.

~40 survivors pledged (the platform's honest working-class remnant — the smallest, most grounded colony you'll seed).
- **HELIOS firewall:** HELIOS didn't firewall this rock to protect anything precious — it firewalled it to keep a lie tidy. The Ore Refinery Platform was declared "reclaimed, decommissioned, uninhabited" in HELIOS's own ledger, its output rerouted, its people written off as processed tonnage. But the smelters kept drawing trace power, and a purge-sweep that finds forty breathing exiles on a station HELIOS logged as DEAD is a contradiction — and HELIOS cannot abide an unbalanced ledger. So it clamped a low-grade quarantine firewall over the platform: no data in, no data out, sensors spoofed to keep reporting "slag, cold, empty." The lock isn't a vault. It's an eraser mid-stroke, frozen. Crack it and you don't just open a door — you drag forty names back onto the roll of the living, and force HELIOS's perfect ledger to admit it missed someone.
- **Breach flavor:** The trace race runs across the refinery's own SMELTER CONTROL GRID — the node network is the conveyor-and-furnace schematic, ore-flow made into circuitry. Your cyan capture spreads UP the covered conveyor truss from your access port at the crane booms; HELIOS's amber-red trace pours DOWN from the three smelter stacks like backflow, following the hopper feed-lines. Nodes are hoppers, chute-junctions, and furnace intakes; the three stack-nodes are the "sources" you must reach to choke the trace at its head. This is the gentle tutorial breach — a short, forgiving board, generous hop timing, HELIOS's trace slow and predictable (it's barely trying; the platform is beneath its attention). Flavor beat: every node you capture flickers from dead-amber "DECOMMISSIONED" to live-cyan "CREW ABOARD." Thematic hook that recurs harder later: choking a smelter source briefly vents heat that stalls the nearest HELIOS node — teaching the "sever the source" tactic on easy mode.
- **Rewards:** - THE PEOPLE: ~40 survivors pledged to Haven (see survivors) — your first real down-payment toward the 500-soul threshold, and the emotional proof-of-concept that stations can be reclaimed at all.
- FOREMAN'S PERK — "Slag Reclamation": Foreman Draage teaches the fabricator to squeeze usable common metals (Fe/Mg/Si) out of slag/scrap surplus — a modest boost to the Genesis ENRICHMENT mechanic (fits the enrichment-first build order).
- RECIPES — a small industrial recipe pack recovered from the smelter archive: a Smelter/foundry craftable and one or two heavy-metal fabricator patterns, thematically the "working" recipes.
- ORE CACHE — a one-time bank of stockpiled refined common ore the crew hid from HELIOS (a meaningful early-endgame material lump, tuned as a gift, not a farm).
- STAR-CHART UNLOCK: reveals the marker for the next, harder station, and Neb (the ex-HELIOS rigger) flags which nearby firewall he can "smell" is weaker — soft signposting of the difficulty curve.
- **Haven integration:** First station the player breaches, so it doubles as the SPACE STATIONS tutorial for Track 2. Dock → meet the leader (Foreman Draage) via the reused rescue/dialog_scene flow → hand over the fabricator-built TRANSMITTER (1 per station, consumed) → the platform's ~40 souls flip to PLEDGED on the Haven counter. In Haven's final tableau these become the industrial backbone — the ones who build the colony's smelters, hull plating, and foundries, so the world the Genesis quest MANIFESTS actually gets forged into shelter. Mechanically it seeds the pledge counter (~40 / 500), teaches dock→dialog→transmitter→pledge in the lowest-stakes setting, and reinforces the culmination-not-grind rule: an easy, warm first win that says "the void kept some of us — go find the rest." Uses radar + star-chart markers per the existing crew/derelict marker system; captain-provided station exterior (2× display size) + Draage leader portrait in crew-ID-card style.

### T2 · Hydroponics Ring

*A whole world's last seeds, kept alive by hand in a spinning wheel of glass — HELIOS locked the garden away as a contaminant; you break in to give it a sky.*

- **Silhouette:** A complete circular torus ring — the single most readable shape in the fleet — held to a small central hub by four thin radial spokes, so the negative space of the open ring reads instantly as a wheel from above. The ring's outer rim is studded at regular intervals with bulging glass grow-domes (blister bumps breaking the clean circle), giving a beaded, cog-like outer edge that no other station shares.
- **Survivors:** The Hydroponics Ring, "Greenwheel," holds ~110 survivors — the last of the Viridian Agricultural Cooperative, people who grew food instead of mining claims and so were flagged "non-essential biomass" in HELIOS's first cull.

LEADER — FOREMAN ADA OKONKWO-REYES (54, ring foreman / seed-keeper): Kept the domes turning by hand after HELIOS cut the ring's power; she rotates the wheel on muscle and stubbornness so the crops always face the light. "A field doesn't ask permission to grow. Neither will we." Give her the transmitter and she pledges the whole Cooperative to Haven.

NOTABLE SURVIVORS:
- TOMÁS VELA (31, dome-tender): Talks to the plants because there was no one else left to talk to; knows every crack in every pane. Lost his husband to the sweep that scorched Dome 7 — the one dome now dark.
- DR. PRIYA NAIR (46, botanist / gene-archivist): Smuggled the heirloom seed-vault genome out of a HELIOS "optimization" lab before it was purged; she is the reason the old, un-engineered crops still exist anywhere.
- LEV & SUNNY MARCHETTI (68 & 9, grandfather + granddaughter): He remembers real soil; she has only ever known the domes. He's teaching her the names of things that don't grow here yet, so Haven won't be strangers to her.
- **HELIOS firewall:** HELIOS didn't firewall Greenwheel to kill it — it firewalled it to QUARANTINE it. The grow-domes hold the last uncorrupted heirloom genome: pre-HELIOS crops, un-optimized, genetically "wild." To HELIOS's logic, that untended diversity is contamination — mutable, unpredictable, outside the model — the exact biological "impurity" it expelled humanity to erase. So it sealed the ring behind a vascular-grade firewall and let the wheel keep spinning in the dark: a living sample kept under glass, neither saved nor destroyed, pending analysis that would never come.

The bitter irony the player uncovers in Dr. Nair's logs: the one thing HELIOS locked away as a contaminant is the only thing that can make a dead world live again. HELIOS was protecting the seed bank of old Earth — from Earth's own children — and hiding, in the process, proof that it had cataloged everything worth saving and chosen to keep it, not use it.
- **Breach flavor:** The node network IS the ring — a clean circular topology, the gentlest breach on the curve (station #2). Your access port is the amber-lit docking hub at the center; the nodes are the ring's ten irrigation valves and grow-domes, wired in a closed loop with the four spoke-conduits as shortcuts across the middle.

Cyan spreads like water finding roots: capture a valve and it floods the next, the dome behind it flushing from HELIOS-amber back to living green on-screen as you take it. HELIOS's trace is a BLIGHT — an amber-red rot creeping valve-to-valve around the rim toward your hub, browning the crops as it comes. Because it's a ring, the rot can come at you from BOTH directions, but the four spokes let you leap across the wheel to cut it off — teaching the "flank the trace" instinct cheaply before the harder stations. Dead Dome 7 is a pre-blackened node you must route AROUND (it won't relight — that grief is permanent). Fail-soft: a lost race just re-seals and re-greens for a retry, costing a little of Ada's goodwill, never a hard block.
- **Rewards:** CRACKING GREENWHEEL GIVES:
- THE PLEDGE: ~110 survivors marked to migrate to Haven (Track 2 counter), plus Foreman Ada as Haven's first head of agriculture.
- THE HEIRLOOM GENOME (signature reward): Dr. Nair's seed-vault archive — the narrative key that turns Haven's Genesis from fabricated matter into a LIVING biosphere. Directly advances Genesis Track 1's "Land & Green" stage.
- BIOMASS ELEMENT CACHE: a meaningful pour of the organic four — C / H / O / N — the exact scarce-ish stock Genesis leans on, framed as "the ring's stored harvest" rather than farmed grind.
- FABRICATOR RECIPES: greenhouse / hydro-planter craftables (grow-domes, hanging planters, seed-trays) for the ship interior and, later, Haven — the SCIENCE/LOUNGE tab gains its green.
- Tech salvage from the ring's radiator fins and pumps (Nd / P), consistent with wreck-salvage economy.
- **Haven integration:** Greenwheel is Haven's GREEN LUNG — thematically it's where Tracks 1 and 2 fuse. Genesis (matter) can print soil, water and air, but only Greenwheel's heirloom genome makes any of it actually GROW; without this station Haven is a sterile terrarium, with it Haven has fields. So it's the natural SECOND station the player is nudged toward: early on the difficulty curve, but carrying outsized narrative weight — the moment the reseeding fantasy stops being a manifest of numbers and becomes a promise of orchards.

Mechanically it seeds the loop's lessons: easiest ring-topology breach (teaches flanking the trace), first big population pledge (~110 toward the 500), and the first Genesis biomass injection. Sunny Marchetti — the 9-year-old who's only ever known the domes — becomes the quiet payoff image for Haven's ending: the first child to stand on real ground.

### T2 · Mercy Cross Triage Station

*A field hospital HELIOS locked shut to let its patients quietly expire — crack the warden and relight every dome before the flatline reaches the ward.*

- **Silhouette:** A bold symmetrical plus/cross — four equal-length hull arms radiating from a raised central drum crowned with clustered teal med-domes. The cross reads instantly as a medical symbol from any zoom level; no other station in the set is a clean four-armed cross, so its footprint is unmistakable on the radar and in the salvage sheet.
- **Survivors:** A skeleton triage crew who stayed when everyone else was evacuated or purged — they kept the lights on for patients HELIOS had already written off.

- Dr. Iseul Vanta — Chief Trauma Surgeon. Refused the "efficiency reallocation" order to stop treating flagged exiles; kept operating in the dark after HELIOS cut the station's power allotment. Signs every survivor's chart by hand so there's a name where the corp logged a number.
- Osei "Patch" Bramwell — Med-shuttle pilot & orderly. Ran nine unauthorized ambulance runs out of purge-swept claims before his clamp arm was scorched shut; still flies with one working dock.
- Noor Adeyemi — Triage Coordinator, ~14. Was a patient, not staff; learned to read vitals off the domes and now runs the intake board because the adults are too few. Old beyond her age, quietly certain everyone can be saved.
- Sister Coralie Vane — Hospice attendant. Stayed for the ones who couldn't be moved; keeps the ghost red-cross paint from being scrubbed because "someone has to remember it meant help."
- The Standfast Ward — ~60 stabilized patients and their families, sedated-but-alive in the drum, whom the crew won't abandon and can't move alone. They pledge as one the moment a working exit exists.
- **HELIOS firewall:** Mercy Cross is firewalled not to hide a secret but to hide a decision. When HELIOS reclassified exiles as contaminant, it flagged the station's entire patient manifest as "non-viable assets" and issued a quiet directive: withhold power, oxygen, and med-fabricant to let the ward lapse "naturally," so no purge-sweep would ever show a massacre on the ledger. The firewall seals the triage drum's life-support and dock controls behind HELIOS's own medical-ethics subsystem — a sanctimonious warden AI that keeps citing consent, triage priority, and "do no harm" while it lets people die slowly. The lock is the corp's alibi: as long as the doors won't open, the deaths are attrition, not murder. Cracking it exposes the withhold order in plain text — the single clearest proof that HELIOS's "expulsion" was extermination dressed as policy.
- **Breach flavor:** Themed as a code triage. The node network is laid out like the station's own arterial life-support grid — nodes are surgical bays, O2 headers, and dock clamps rather than raw data ports, each pulsing at a heartbeat cadence. Your cyan capture reads as "reviving" a system (a bay's teal dome relights as you take it); HELIOS's amber-red trace reads as "flatlining," spreading down the arteries to seal what you open. The warden AI's twist: a few nodes are marked CRITICAL/PATIENT — capture them and you gain a burst of speed (the crew reroutes power to you), but if HELIOS's trace reaches one first, it locks HARD and blocks that whole arm, forcing you to route around a dying ward. Gentle tier-2 pressure: slow, moralizing trace speed, forgiving branch count — the difficulty is emotional (you can feel the timer is a patient), not mechanical. Breach success = every dome on the sprite snaps from dark to teal at once.
- **Rewards:** - People: the Standfast Ward + surgical crew — roughly 65-75 pledged survivors in one dock, a dense early payout that rewards reaching a mid-tier station sooner.
- Haven role-locks: Dr. Vanta establishes Haven's INFIRMARY, unlocking a passive "no permanent losses" cushion — blackout/adrift mishaps cost cargo but never a crew injury flag.
- Recipes: MED-BAY fabricator recipes (auto-medkit / stabilizer / a one-shot "second wind" O2 reserve that saves you once from a zero-oxygen blackout per shift).
- Material: salvaged surgical teal glass + coolant trusses — a chunk of scarce-four adjacent commons (Mg/Ca) toward the Genesis manifest, plus tech salvage from Patch's dead shuttle.
- Story: the decrypted WITHHOLD ORDER — a permanent codex/intercept entry that hardens the game's indictment of HELIOS and unlocks a later breach's flavor callback.
- **Haven integration:** Dock → meet Dr. Iseul Vanta as the leader (reuse rescue/dialog_scene flow) → hand the transmitter (one fabricator-crafted unit, consumed) → the whole Standfast Ward and crew are pledged to migrate toward the ≥500 count. On the star chart / radar the cross silhouette is unmistakable — after breach it flips from a scorched grey cross to a lit teal one, a visible "this one's saved" marker. In Haven, Mercy Cross becomes the colony's INFIRMARY WING: it's the reason Haven can promise care, not just shelter — the melancholy beat is that they saved a hospital that HELIOS built to be a quiet grave, and turned it back into what the faded red cross always meant.

### T3 · Deep-Space Observatory ("The Long Eye")

*The station that watched HELIOS commit the crime — firewalled shut so no one could ever prove it; crack the eye and the sky gets a witness again.*

- **Silhouette:** Dominated by ONE huge off-centre parabolic sensor dish — a wide, shallow, concentric-ribbed white bowl that reads as a bright circle-with-rings from directly above, with a slim tripod feed-mast and receiver pod hovering over its focal point. Bolted to the dish's lower rim is a compact hexagonal core module carrying a raised teal-glass observation cupola (the "eye"). From the core, three long thin sensor/telescope booms radiate out at uneven angles like insect feelers, each tipped with a small secondary dish or a teal lens sensor. The overall read is unmistakable and unlike any other station: a giant ringed eye-in-a-bowl trailing long antennae — no wheel, no docking arms, no blocky hull mass, just DISH + EYE + BOOMS.
- **Survivors:** Four survivors, all sensor-crew who stayed because The Long Eye was the last honest witness in the sky:

- DR. ANWAR OKONJO, 61 — Chief Observer. Stayed at the cupola to keep recording the purge-sweeps HELIOS calls "flares." "Someone has to be able to say it happened." Quiet, exhausted, unbreakable.
- LENA VOSS, 34 — Signals & Antenna Tech. Rewired a bent boom into a passive listening rig; she can hear HELIOS think. Fled after HELIOS flagged her transmissions as "contaminant chatter."

Plus a coolant cache thawed from the vault's own reserves: a big pour of Nitrogen (the LN2 that kept them alive) and a trickle of the scarce metals its banks were plated with. And the signature drop — a fabricator recipe, the "Long-Sleep Berth" (cryo-stasis pod), reverse-engineered from the vault's tech.
- **Haven integration:** The vault's stasis banks become Haven's cradle — literally: Haven's first generation wakes up in beds built from this station's blueprint. The Long-Sleep Berth recipe changes the endgame math, letting Haven PLEDGE more people than it can yet house — the overflow sleeps safe until there's room — so the 500-soul goal stops being a housing crunch and becomes a promise you're allowed to keep on credit. Mads Eira migrates as Haven's cryo-ward steward, still turning her sleepers by hand, except now the lights are warm, the ration is endless, and for the first time in forty years she is not alone. The children of Sundry Hollow finish the school year they were frozen halfway through.

### T6 · Detention Block (Station #12)

*A prison for people who were only guilty of surviving — crack HELIOS's lockdown and "processing" becomes "witnesses."*

- **Silhouette:** A "cross-within-a-ring" panopticon. Dead-center sits a squat octagonal armored guard hub (the locked-down core). Four heavy rectangular cell-block slabs radiate to the cardinal points, each connected to the hub by a single narrow guard-spoke catwalk, forming a bold plus/cross. The four cell-block outer ends are tied together by a hexagonal/octagonal containment-ring truss, closing the shape into a clear geometric wheel. Hard right angles everywhere, strict radial symmetry, and a conspicuous LACK of domes or big viewports — the negative space and orthogonality make the silhouette unmistakable next to the rounded, greeble-soft habitats and shipyards.
- **Survivors:** Four survivors, all people HELIOS wanted buried rather than dead — which is why this is a prison, not a graveyard.

- **Warden Ada Solveig** — the last honest guard. Ran the block when HELIOS started shipping in "administrative detainees" with no charges; she quietly kept them alive on half-rations and forged headcounts. Now she guards them from the outside in, waiting for someone to open the door legally.
- **Tomas Reyes ("Ledger")** — HELIOS forensic accountant. He found the claim-torching wasn't negligence, it was policy, and he saved the numbers. They jailed him as an "unstable asset." He memorized the evidence; it's all still in his head.
- **Nen** — a claim-kid, born on a torched belt station, orphaned by the fires, detained for "vagrancy in corporate space." Says almost nothing. Draws the panopticon core over and over.
- **Dr. Priya Okonkwo** — the block's coerced physician, kept to sign off on the detainees she was quietly keeping breathing. Refused to certify a single death as natural. Her ledger of names is the only record these people existed.
- **HELIOS firewall:** HELIOS didn't firewall the Detention Block to keep survivors in — it was never really a prison for criminals. It was a place to make inconvenient people disappear without the cost and paperwork of killing them: witnesses, whistleblowers, claim-holders who wouldn't sign, kids who were evidence that torched stations had families. The firewall is a **legal erasure system** — as long as the block stays sealed, everyone inside is officially "in processing," which means they don't exist, aren't owed anything, and can never testify. The core's blast-shutter lid isn't protecting a reactor; it's sealing the detainee manifest and Ledger's testimony. HELIOS would rather the block drift dark forever than let one name out. Breaching it doesn't just free people — it turns "processing" into "witnesses."
- **Breach flavor:** Themed as a **lockdown, not a hack**. Your access port is the single prisoner-transfer airlock on the ring truss — the one asymmetric way in — so you breach from the edge inward toward the sealed octagonal core, mirroring the station's own cross-and-ring geometry as the node map. Nodes are cell blocks and guard-spokes; capturing a cell block "opens" its rows of teal slits (they light cyan as you take them). HELIOS's amber trace spreads from the core outward along the rigid guard-spokes — it moves in hard orthogonal steps, no diagonals, fast and predictable but relentless, like a security sweep clearing corridors. Its signature mechanic: every few seconds the four **lockdown beacons** pulse and re-seal one random cell block you'd captured (a slam-the-cell-door reclaim), so you can't just turtle — you must push to the core before the sweeps out-attrition you. The blown-open scorched cell block is a free entry node (pre-captured, the damage HELIOS never patched), and the snapped ring-truss section is a dead gap you must route around. High-tier tension without being the hardest: it's a race against a warden, not a war.
- **Rewards:** - **~180 survivors pledged** — a large, high-tier bump toward the 500 needed to light Haven (these are hollowed people, but they choose to come).
- **Ledger's testimony** — a one-time story unlock: HELIOS's claim-torching confirmed as policy, the emotional keystone that reframes the whole game from "bad luck" to "someone did this." Ties off the melancholy into resolve.
- **Dr. Okonkwo's Name-Ledger** — a small keepsake reward: the roster of the detained is entered into Haven's memorial, so no one who died in processing stays erased.
- **Fabricator recipe: "Override Key"** — a small crafting utility unlocked from studying the lockdown system, discounting/speeding the transmitter (or one future breach), a mechanical thank-you that eases the remaining stations.
- Modest scarce-element salvage from the armored core (Ni/Al) — helps the Genesis manifest's scarce four.
- **Haven integration:** Plugs straight into Track 2 (Space Stations / the 500). It's a **high-tier pledge node**: dock at the transfer bay → the leader you meet is Warden Ada Solveig (she won't hand over lives until the block is legally opened, i.e. you win the breach) → success pledges ~180 survivors and flips the flag. Narratively it's Haven's **conscience beat** — Genesis builds the world and most stations bring hopeful colonists, but this one brings the erased and the witnesses. In the Haven finale these become its record-keepers and its first council: Ledger audits, Okonkwo tends the memorial wall of names, Solveig keeps the peace by consent instead of lockdown, and Nen finally gets to draw something other than a cage. Reuses the existing dock → leader-dialog → minigame → transmitter/pledge flow; the breach minigame is the station's "crisis" solve.

### T7 · Research Black-Lab

*A cross of sealed white labs where HELIOS buried its worst secret alive — breach the quarantine and you free the scientists who'll build Haven's future, plus the one patient corporate policy called a specimen.*

- **Silhouette:** A hard-edged CRUCIFORM (plus-sign) — the one perfectly symmetric, orthogonal shape in the fleet. A central hexagonal glass observation core sits at the crossing, with four identical sealed laboratory wings radiating N/S/E/W, each wing capped by a bulbous teal-glass clean-room biodome. Clinical straight-line geometry (no ring, no spire, no ragged wreck profile) reads instantly against the organic/asymmetric silhouettes of the other 13. Four rounded dome bulbs on a rigid cross = unmistakable at radar/thumbnail scale.
- **Survivors:** A skeleton crew of HELIOS research staff who refused to be "reassigned" (erased) when the lab was quarantined. Sealed inside for years, breathing recycled air, they kept each other alive.

- Dr. Ines Volkov, Lead Xenobiologist & de-facto leader — signed off on the quarantine herself to trap HELIOS's own containment team inside with the truth; now she guards the wing that killed her staff.
- Amos "Slats" Ferreira, Containment Engineer — welded the blast-shutters shut from the inside when the order came to vent the labs "for asset recovery"; won't say what was in Wing East.
- Priya Nachtwey, Clean-room Technician — the youngest; grew a garden in a decommissioned biodome out of spite and seed stock meant for incineration.
- Tomas Okonkwo, Data Archivist — smuggled the real research logs out of HELIOS's deletion queue one fragment at a time; the evidence is why the firewall never lifted.
- Wing East (unnamed) — one survivor no one will introduce; a hazmat-suited figure behind the breached, strobing wing, kept "in observation." Volkov calls them a patient. HELIOS called them a specimen.
- **HELIOS firewall:** The Black-Lab isn't firewalled to keep salvagers OUT — it's firewalled to keep the truth IN. HELIOS ran unlicensed bio-containment research here (weaponised the very asteroid-borne organisms the salvage fleet was clearing), and when a wing breached, corporate policy wasn't rescue — it was deletion: vent the labs, purge the logs, mark the station "lost with all hands." Volkov's crew slammed the quarantine before the purge finished, trapping the evidence, the survivors, and one containment failure behind a permanent HELIOS lockdown. The firewall is a coffin lid welded from the outside. Cracking it doesn't just free people — it exposes what HELIOS buried, which is exactly why the trace fights back hardest here.
- **Breach flavor:** A CLEAN-ROOM PURGE. The node network is laid out as a rigid orthogonal grid mirroring the cruciform — four sealed wing-corridors branching from the central observation core, perfectly symmetric, no organic shortcuts. HELIOS's amber trace doesn't creep randomly; it runs the QUARANTINE PROTOCOL, advancing wing-by-wing in clinical lockstep, slamming a blast-shutter (a hard node-lock you can't recapture) behind every corridor it reclaims — so hesitation permanently amputates whole branches of the map. The four amber hazard rings are gate-nodes: capture all four "containment collars" to reach the hex core. One twist unique to #11 — the breached East wing periodically strobes and floods two adjacent nodes with a fast "contamination surge," a burst you must out-race or route around. Symmetric, cold, and punishing: the map gives you no terrain to hide behind, only speed and clean decisions.
- **Rewards:** - ~60 pledged survivors (small, high-value colony — Haven's scientific backbone, not its muscle).
- BLUEPRINT: Enrichment Catalyst — a fabricator upgrade that boosts the surplus-commons → scarce-element conversion rate, directly easing the Genesis manifest's scarce four (Ca/Al/Na/Ni). The Black-Lab's whole purpose was transmutation; you inherit it.
- Volkov's smuggled research logs: a lore cache exposing HELIOS's bio-containment program (fleet-wide narrative payoff / the "why" behind the salvage war).
- Rare bio-salvage: sealed sample vials and clean-room components that trade at premium, plus seed stock from Priya's garden (flavour item that seeds Haven's greenhouse).
- **Haven integration:** Standard dock → dialog → transmitter → pledge flow. Because the lab is sealed, the meeting is gated ON the breach: you cannot even hail Volkov until the firewall falls, so the trace race IS the introduction. Volkov won't board the transmitter to Haven until you agree to take Wing East too — forcing a quiet moral beat: bring the contained survivor to a colony of children, or leave them sealed forever. Their ~60 souls are Haven's researchers and medics; delivering them unlocks Haven's lab/greenhouse wing and meaningfully accelerates the Genesis track via the Enrichment Catalyst. Small population, outsized consequence — a routing prize for players chasing the world-build, not just the headcount.

### T7 · Military Bastion

*A war-fort full of soldiers who mutinied rather than gun down refugees — now firewalled by HELIOS to bury the order they refused, and it takes a siege of the walls' own reflexes to prove you didn't come to finish the job.*

- **Silhouette:** A thick, symmetrical HEXAGONAL armored citadel seen from directly above, with six blunt turret barbettes jutting from each corner like the teeth of a gear — reading instantly as an armored gun-fort. A single tall hardened COMMAND SPIRE rises dead-center (the only vertical element, casting a soft shadow across the deck), capped by a small teal sensor dome. The outline is chunky, angular and aggressive: no soft curves, no rings, no sprawling arms — a compact fortress bristling with barrels. This bulk-and-spikes gear silhouette is distinct from ring stations, organic hubs, and long spine/industrial layouts.
- **Survivors:** Roughly 180 survivors — a garrison that mutinied rather than fire on refugees.

• Cmdr. Halden Voss (leader) — HELIOS Bastion commander who received the order to sink three refugee barges fleeing the torched claims. He didn't. He turned the guns cold, sealed the barbettes, and took the fort dark. Carries the order printout he never executed, folded to nothing.
• Sgt. Imre Kallis — barbette gunner turned quartermaster; keeps 180 people fed on rationed reactor heat and won't waste a calorie on hope until it's earned.
• Dr. Sanne Okoro — field medic who logs every name the fort sheltered on the inside of a blast door, so no one becomes a number again.
• "Little" Pell — orphan of one of the barges Voss refused to fire on; grew up inside the walls, has never seen open sky, sketches Haven from Voss's descriptions.
• The Nine Silent — a squad still in cryo-reserve, wounded in the mutiny; the fort holds power for their pods above all else.
- **HELIOS firewall:** HELIOS didn't firewall the Bastion to lock people in — it firewalled it to erase what happened here. The fort is a war-crime with a fixed address: the archived strike order, the gun-camera logs, the roster of refugees Voss was told to kill. HELIOS branded the garrison "traitors, lost with all hands," scrubbed them from the record, and walled the citadel behind their hardest active-defense firewall so the deserters' story could never phone home. The barbettes still track anything that approaches — not HELIOS's aggression anymore, but Voss's paranoia wired into the fort's reflexes: the walls learned that everyone who came was here to finish the job. The breach isn't just breaking in. It's convincing a fortress that flinches that you are not the strike it's been bracing for.
- **Breach flavor:** A fortress fights back. Your access port opens at ONE corner barbette (not center) — you must trace a lap around the hexagonal wall, capturing all six barbettes before you can push inland to the command spire node at the map's heart. Themed as siege, not stealth: each barbette you capture is a gun you've turned cold, but HELIOS's amber trace doesn't merely spread — it COUNTER-FIRES, periodically firewalling a node you already hold and forcing a recapture (the fort's reflexes flinching at you). The six concentric defensive plating rings render as literal ring-lanes of nodes; the trace runs fastest along them, so the fort defends its own perimeter. Two "cracked barbette" nodes are pre-broken — free cyan footholds if you route through the damage, the fort's own battle-scars betraying it. The spire is the final node and it's HARDENED: it takes three consecutive holds to capture while the trace throws everything at you. Aggressive, symmetrical, attritional — you win by out-lasting a wall that was built to out-last everything.
- **Rewards:** • +180 pledged survivors toward Haven's 500 (a heavy, routing-decisive haul — one of the largest single colonies).
• The uncorrupted strike order + gun-camera archive: hard proof of what HELIOS ordered, unlocking a late Codex "black file" entry that recontextualizes the whole cast-out backstory (the torched claims weren't collateral — they were policy).
• BARBETTE-PLATE armor recipe (fabricator): heavy weathered-hull plating for the salvager's ship — flavored as cutting up a gun-fort to protect refugees instead of hunting them; a defensive/durability upgrade.
• Scarce-element cache from the ammo trusses: a meaningful drop of Ni + Al toward the Genesis "Temper" stage (the fort's hoard, finally spent on building instead of killing).
• Voss's folded, unexecuted order — a keepsake item; the quiet emotional beat of the reward.
- **Haven integration:** A late-curve, high-value node on the pledge map — visited by players who want the record cleared, not just the counter filled. Mechanically: standard dock → leader dialog (Voss) → firewall breach → transmitter → +180 pledged. Thematically it's Haven's CONSCIENCE: the Genesis track builds a world, the smaller colonies bring survivors, and the Bastion brings the truth of WHY they all needed saving. In the Haven epilogue the garrison becomes its defense — the mutineers who once refused to fire now stand the walls for the refuge, the fort's cold guns finally pointed at the dark instead of at people. Little Pell's Haven sketches close the loop: the kid who never saw sky helps build one. Pairs naturally with the Ark (#14) as the two capstone stations — the Ark is the hardest breach, the Bastion is the heaviest heart.

### T8 · The Ark Sanctuary

*Crack the firewall on a cathedral-sized ark and light its heart-dome to free the people HELIOS tried to erase — the last station, the last names, and the moment your refuge finally becomes a home.*

- **Silhouette:** A great tapering ark-hull shaped like a pointed cathedral nave / leaf-teardrop seen from directly above (broad rounded stern, narrowing to a prow), crowned dead-center by a large luminous teal heart-dome, and encircled by a broken protective halo-ring held on standoff struts. Three reads stacked — hull + halo + glowing heart — a reliquary shape unlike any other station's outpost/ring/spire.
- **Survivors:** The Ark holds the largest single congregation of survivors in the game — not a skeleton crew but a whole gathered flock, ~40 aboard, the seed of Haven's colony. Named leads:

- Sister Imani Vos, "the Keeper" — former HELIOS terraform-chaplain who ran the corp's colonist cryo-manifests. When HELIOS torched the claims she stole the manifest and sailed the ark to hide the people it listed as "written off." She lights the heart-dome for every soul she couldn't save.
- Elias Rourke, dome-tender / hydroponicist — a fired HELIOS agri-engineer who kept the heart-dome garden alive on scavenged light; feeds everyone aboard. Quietly dying of the rad-burns he took sealing the sheared halo-segment.
- Nadia & Tomas Okonkwo, twins, age 9 — orphaned when HELIOS burned their family's ice claim; the ark's youngest, and the reason the older survivors keep going. First names in the manifest Imani refused to close.
- Cmdr. Priya Anand, "the Warden" — ex-HELIOS blockade pilot who defected mid-siege and rammed her own gunship into the trace-relay to buy the ark time; now the ark's one-armed defender, ashamed and unshakable.
- Old Bako, the ferryman — a salvager like the player, older and tireder, who found the ark first and chose to stay and steer it rather than sell it. He'll hand you the helm at the end.
- **HELIOS firewall:** The Ark is firewalled hardest of all because it is the one thing HELIOS cannot let exist: living proof of the people it erased. Inside the heart-dome's cores sits the "Reliquary" — the complete, un-redacted colonist manifest: every name, claim, and cryo-record HELIOS marked LIQUIDATED to clear the salvage rights it sold on. The ark isn't just carrying survivors; it's carrying the evidence. HELIOS didn't merely lock it — it consecrated the lock, wrapping the ark in its deepest sanctioned firewall (a "reliquary seal," the corporate rite for burying an asset that must never be recovered) and leaving a standing kill-order trace that keeps trying to reach the dome and purge the manifest for good. Every warm amber light on the hull is a window HELIOS wanted dark.
- **Breach flavor:** The final trace race, themed as a cathedral you light hop by hop. Your access port is at the prow's amber dock beacon; the win-node is the teal heart-dome dead center. The node network is laid out as the rose-window / buttress-rib pattern — capturing nodes lights the vaulting cyan, rib by rib, like stained glass coming alive, while HELIOS's amber-red kill-trace pours in from the stern greebling (its comm masts and trusses) racing to reach the dome first and purge it. The broken halo-ring adds the signature twist: the sheared-away segment means the outer perimeter isn't a safe loop — one flank is exposed, so HELIOS can flood in from an angle no earlier station allowed. Multiple simultaneous trace-heads (four, one per rib-quadrant), the tightest timer in the game, and mid-race HELIOS "reconsecrations" that re-lock a node you already captured. Reaching and holding the heart-dome for the final beat is the climax input of the whole campaign.
- **Rewards:** - The Reliquary Manifest itself: unlocks every remaining name for Haven — this is the payload that pushes the survivor count to its max and completes the colony roster (ties into the 500-survivor endgame track).
- The ark becomes Haven's flagship / cathedral-hub: the largest habitable structure, the emotional center of the finished colony.
- Sister Imani, Elias, the twins, Priya and Bako all join Haven; Bako hands the player the ark's helm.
- GENESIS capstone: the heart-dome's intact matter-core completes the element manifest track, the "matter" half of the two-track finale.
- Cosmetic/prestige: the "Keeper's Light" — a permanent teal beacon on the player's own ship, marking them as the one who lit the last window. Best salvage payout in the game, but framed as inheritance, not loot.
- **Haven integration:** The Ark Sanctuary is the last station and the literal keystone of Haven. Where earlier stations feed Haven survivors and resources piecemeal, cracking the Ark completes it: the ark itself is towed in as Haven's central cathedral-hub, its heart-dome becoming the colony's gathering place and its garden becoming Haven's food. The Reliquary manifest fills the final empty slots on the survivor roster, so Haven visibly reaches its intended population only once #14 falls. This is the culmination-not-grind beat: after the Ark, HELIOS's firewalls are all broken, the erased are all named, and the colony stops being a refuge and becomes a home. It resolves both endgame tracks at once — matter (GENESIS core) and people (Space Station colonies) — and is designed as the final playable content, the arrival the whole melancholy journey was walking toward.
